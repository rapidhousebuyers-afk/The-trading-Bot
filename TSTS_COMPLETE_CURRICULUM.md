### OPENING HOOK:

Before you can trade the system, you have to speak the language. Every indicator on your chart has a specific job. By the end of this chapter, you'll know exactly what each one is telling you.

### SECTION 1 — THE BOKK:

The Bokk is your institutional flow indicator. It shows up on your chart as a cloud or bar that tapers and expands. When the Bokk opens up, institutional momentum is entering the market. When it tapers or closes, that momentum is exhausting. This often leads to a pullback or a Blue Line trade. Think of it as the fuel gauge for the move.

[INSERT IMAGE: Chart showing Bokk open vs Bokk tapering/closed]

### SECTION 2 — YELLOW LINE:

The Yellow Line is a primary Exponential Moving Average. This is your entry trigger line. In a GGG RGG setup, you wait for the Reset to touch or interact with this line before you execute the trade. If the Yellow Line isn't involved, it's not a Safety Trade. In Chapter 7 you'll learn that the Yellow Line is also your conservative entry point.

[INSERT IMAGE: Chart showing Yellow Line on candle chart]

### SECTION 3 — GOLD LINE:

The Gold Line is your reversal warning line. It sits at a higher structural level than the Yellow Line. When price interacts with the Gold Line, you're looking at a potential trend shift or a deep reset. This is where high-probability entries live. In Chapter 7 you'll learn that the Gold Line is also your aggressive entry point. Respect the Gold Line.

[INSERT IMAGE: Chart showing Gold Line on candle chart]

### SECTION 4 — BS DETECTOR:

BS stands for Bull/Sell. This is your momentum bias indicator. Here's how to read it:

- **Bright Green or Bright Red** means strong, active momentum. The move is alive.
- **Dimming** means you're in the Reset phase. That's the R in GGG RGG.
- **When the BS curves away from the Yellow Line**, it creates a Gap. That Gap signals momentum exhaustion.

The BS detector is your early warning system. Learn to read the brightness and the dimming. That's where the trade setup lives.

[INSERT IMAGE: Chart showing BS bright vs dimming vs gap]

### SECTION 5 — ORDER TYPES (BUY STOP AND SELL STOP):

Before we go further, you need to understand two order types that you'll use in every single trade.

A **Buy Stop** is an order placed above the current price. It triggers a buy when price reaches that level. You use this to confirm momentum is resuming upward before you commit to the trade. You're not guessing. You're waiting for price to prove it.

A **Sell Stop** is an order placed below the current price. It triggers a sell when price reaches that level. You use this to confirm momentum is resuming downward. Same logic — wait for proof.

SS stands for Sell Stop. It's the exact opposite of the Buy Stop. When the BS gives you a buy signal, the SS confirms the sell side.

In Chapter 7 you'll learn exactly where to place these orders using the Gold Line and Yellow Line.

### SECTION 6 — HISTO3:

Histo3 is your histogram. It shows the V patterns and Mountain patterns on your chart. This is primarily how you identify the Logo Trade. Histo3 tracks the strength and direction of the immediate move and confirms when a pullback has completed its Reset.

[INSERT IMAGE: Chart showing Histo3 histogram patterns]

### SECTION 7 — 3LINES (DIRECTIONAL BARS):

The 3lines sit at the bottom of your chart, below the BS. These are your Directional Bars. They represent the locked-in trend of the higher timeframes.

- **Solid Green** means macro bullish bias. The higher timeframes are in your favor.
- **Solid Red** means macro bearish bias. The higher timeframes are against the trend.
- **Mixed or chop** means no-trade zone. The timeframes are in disagreement. Stay out.

[INSERT IMAGE: Chart showing Directional Bars — green vs red vs mixed]

### SECTION 8 — PINK LINES:

The Pink Lines are your faster-moving momentum trackers. When these lines are pinned at the top between 80 and 100, or pinned at the bottom between 0 and 20, the move is overextended. That's your signal that a pullback is coming.

[INSERT IMAGE: Chart showing Pink Lines pinned at extremes]

### SECTION 9 — PURPLE LINE:

The Purple Line often coincides with the actual candle movement. It tracks the real-time price action closely.

### SECTION 10 — BLUE LINE:

The Blue Line is your slower anchor momentum line. In Chapter 4 we'll go deep on how the Blue Line acts as gravity. For now, just know it exists and it's important.

[INSERT IMAGE: Chart showing Blue Line on chart]

### SECTION 11 — UNO REVERSE (KEITH BOT):

The Uno Reverse, also known as the Keith Bot, is a multi-timeframe convergence dashboard. It monitors five major indices — US30, NAS100, NQ1!, SPX500, and ES1! — across sequential timeframes. Its job is to identify when a market reversal is occurring, specifically from bearish red to bullish green. This is built for the overnight reversal trade setup. Think of it as a domino detector. When the timeframes start flipping from red to green in ascending order, a reversal is in progress. We'll cover exactly how to trade it in Chapter 6.

[INSERT IMAGE: Screenshot of the Keith Bot dashboard showing all five indices with color-coded status]

Now you know every component on your chart and every order type you'll use. Each one has a job. Each one tells you something specific. Next chapter, we put them all together into the trade that changed everything — GGG RGG.

### SECTION 1 — WHAT IT IS:

The Uno Reverse, also called the Keith Bot, is a semi-automatic robot indicator. It monitors five major indices — **US30, NAS100, NQ1!, SPX500, and ES1!** — across sequential timeframes. It reads whether price is above or below the system's Blue Line (an EMA) on each timeframe for each symbol.

**Green** means price is above the Blue Line. **Red** means price is below.

The display uses color-coded triangles — green pointing up, red pointing down — so you can see the entire market picture at a glance.

The Keith Bot is named in honor of the great late **Keith Palmer**, the coder who built it. Kevin describes it as "a big sniper rifle on steroids" — it reads the Sniper indicator data and condenses all the information into one dashboard, then sends an automatic signal when the setup triggers.

Its job is to identify when a market reversal is occurring, specifically from bearish to bullish. This is designed for the overnight reversal trade setup — what Kevin calls **"The Only Trade You'll Ever Need."**

### SECTION 2 — THE SEQUENTIAL FLIP (DOMINO EFFECT):

Here's how you read the dashboard. During a reversal, the timeframes flip from red to green **in ascending order**:

1. 1-Second turns green first
2. Then the 5-Second
3. Then the 10-Second
4. Then the 15-Second
5. Then the 30-Second
6. Then the 45-Second
7. Then the 1-Minute
8. Then the 2-Minute

10. ...and continues upward

Each flip confirms the reversal is gaining strength. It's a domino effect. If the 1s, 5s, and 10s have flipped, you **know** the 15s is next. This predictability is what allows you to call the trade before the move completes.

### SECTION 3 — THE OREO COOKIE TRADE (OREO SANDWICH PATTERN):

There's a visual pattern you'll learn to recognize. We call it the **Oreo Cookie Trade** — or the Oreo Sandwich.

**The Top Cookie:** The higher timeframes — 15-minute, 30-minute, 1-hour, Daily — are already green. The macro trend has already turned.

**The Cream Filling:** The middle timeframes are still red. They're being compressed and overwhelmed from both sides.

**The Bottom Cookie:** The lower timeframes — 5-second, 10-second, 15-second — are turning green. Micro momentum is pushing up.

When you see this pattern, momentum is compressing from both sides. The reversal is loading. The dominoes are about to fall.

[INSERT IMAGE: Dashboard showing the Oreo Sandwich pattern — green on top and bottom, red in the middle]

### SECTION 4 — THE LAGGING SYMBOL ENTRY RULE:

This is where the trade lives.

The setup occurs when **four of the five indices show green** on your trigger timeframe, **but one remains red**. That one symbol is lagging behind.

**Here's the example:** On the 15-second timeframe, US30, NAS, NQ1, and SPX are all green. But ES1 is still red.

**The action:** Enter a **buy stop on the lagging symbol** — in this case ES1. Target its 15-second Blue Line.

**The logic:** "When one goes up, they all go up." The lagging symbol will catch up to the others. You're buying the last domino before it falls.

The Keith Bot isn't just a visual dashboard — it fires an **automatic signal** when the setup triggers on the fourth timeframe. The signal can be configured to display on screen, send to email, or push to your phone via TradingView alert integration.

You don't have to watch every timeframe manually. The robot tells you when to enter.

**The trigger rule:** When US30 is over its fourth time period AND either NAS or SPX is too — leaving one symbol lagging — the robot sends the signal.

Every TSTS trade has two phases. Phase one is setup confirmation. That's what you learned in Chapters 3 through 6. You check the bottom-up confluence — Directional Bars, BS Reset, Bokk Opening, Candle EMA. Phase two is execution. That's this chapter. Once the setup confirms, you place your order using these exact steps.

### SECTION 2 — SETTING THE ENTRY ORDER:

Once the bottom-up confluence is met — Directional Bars locked, BS showing Reset, Bokk Opening in trend direction — you place your entry order.

**For Longs:** Place a Buy Stop at the Gold Line for an aggressive entry. Or place a Buy Stop at the Yellow Line for a conservative entry.

**For Shorts:** Place a Sell Stop at the Gold Line for an aggressive entry. Or place a Sell Stop at the Yellow Line for a conservative entry.

### SECTION 3 — AGGRESSIVE VS CONSERVATIVE:

The aggressive entry at the Gold Line gets you in earlier. You catch more of the move. But there's slightly more risk because price hasn't pulled back as far.

The conservative entry at the Yellow Line gets you in later. You catch less of the move. But there's less risk because price has pulled back further and proven itself.

Neither is wrong. It depends on your risk tolerance and how confident you are in the setup.

### SECTION 4 — STOP LOSS PLACEMENT:

Place your stop loss just above or below the swing point — the candle structure — where the trend reversed during the reset.

For longs, the stop goes just below that swing low.

For shorts, the stop goes just above that swing high.

Here's why: If price breaks that structure, the momentum alignment is officially invalid. The safety of the trade is gone. That's your line in the sand.

### SECTION 5 — TAKE PROFIT BY TRADE TYPE:

Different trades have different targets. Here's the breakdown:

**The Safety Trade (GGG RGG):** Target the previous local high for longs or the previous local low for shorts. Alternatively, target the Blue Line on the higher timeframe. These are the next major liquidity zones where price typically stalls.

**The Blue Line Trade:** Target the Blue Line on the execution timeframe — the 30-second or 1-minute. This is the mean. The mathematical point where market gravity is satisfied. Don't get greedy. Take the Blue Line.

**The Logo Trade:** There is no fixed price target. You exit when the BS and/or the candles lose overall momentum. Since this is a high-convection move, you let it run until the lower timeframe color turns. If you're trading the 5-minute, watch the 30-second. When the 30-second loses its bright color, the move is exhausted. Get out.

**The Uno Reverse (Keith Bot):** Target the Blue Line of the timeframe where the signal triggered. If the signal was on the 15-second, your target is the 15-second Blue Line. If momentum continues, it may push to the next timeframe's Blue Line.

### SECTION 8 — TRADING PSYCHOLOGY:

This is the part most traders skip. Don't skip it.

**Rule 1 — Follow the system.** You built the checklist for a reason. If all four layers don't confirm, there is no trade. Walking away is a winning decision.

**Rule 2 — Respect your stop.** Once the stop is placed, it stays. Moving your stop is not "managing the trade." It's gambling. If price hits your stop, the trade is over. Move on.

**Rule 3 — Don't chase.** If you miss the entry, you missed it. The Uno Reverse chapter proved this — by the time the 30-second turns green, the move is already complete. There will be another trade. There's always another trade.

**Rule 4 — Size your risk.** Never risk more than you can afford to lose on a single trade. The system is called The Safety Trade System for a reason. Safety comes first.

**Rule 5 — Detach from the outcome.** You can do everything right and still lose a trade. That's the market. What matters is that you followed the system. Over time, the system wins.
